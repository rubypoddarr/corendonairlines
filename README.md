2. Check-in Information
Online Check-in: Typically opens 72 hours before departure and closes 3 to 5 hours before.
Airport Arrival: Desks usually open 2.5 to 3 hours before departure and close 60 minutes before take-off.
Boarding Gate: You must be at the gate at least 25 minutes before your flight departs. 
3. Baggage Requirements
Cabin Baggage: Standard size is 55x40x25 cm with a weight limit of 8kg to 10kg, depending on your fare type.
Underseat Bag: In "Promo" or "Lite" fares, only a small personal item (max 40x30x20 cm) that fits under the seat is free.
Checked Baggage: Allowance varies by fare (e.g., 23kg for Flex, 2x20kg for Premium). Any single piece of luggage cannot exceed 32kg.
Liquids: Must be in containers of 100ml or less, placed in a single, transparent, resealable 1-litre plastic bag. 
4. Special Services (If Applicable)
Unaccompanied Minors: Available for children aged 6 to 12 years; a Discharge of Responsibility form and a fee (approx. €70) are required.
Pets: Traveling with a pet in the cabin requires a pet passport, microchip, and health certificates.
Disability Assistance: Free medical assistance should be requested at least 48 hours before the flight. 

2. Boarding Pass & Airport Fees

Mandatory Digital Check-in: For many airports, online check-in is mandatory. If you choose not to check in online, you may be charged an airport check-in fee of approximately €10.
Printed vs. Mobile: You can use a mobile boarding pass at most major airports. However, at certain locations (e.g., Hurghada, Kayseri, Adana), you must still visit the check-in counter to obtain a printed boarding pass even if you checked in online.
Entry Requirements: Some destinations, like Curaçao, require a "Digital Immigration Card" (DI Card) to be completed online starting 7 days before departure; a printout of this card is required for entry. 
2. Pregnancy & Family Travel
Pregnancy Limits: You can fly without a doctor's report until the 36th week (or 32nd week for multiple pregnancies). Beyond these limits, travel is not permitted even with a medical certificate.
Infant Baggage: Infants (under 2 years) are entitled to 10kg of checked baggage on all flights. You may also bring one stroller, car seat, or pushchair free of charge, which will be stored in the hold.
Seat Restrictions: Pregnant passengers and those traveling with infants are not permitted to sit in extra legroom or emergency exit rows for safety reasons. 
3. Special Equipment & Assistance
Sports & Music: Musical instruments smaller than 25x117x38 cm and under 8kg can be carried as hand luggage; larger items go in the cargo hold and count toward your baggage allowance.
Medical Devices: Portable oxygen concentrators and ventilators are allowed with a medical report dated within one week of the flight. CPAP (sleep apnea) devices do not require a medical report.
Wheelchairs: Manual or electric wheelchairs are carried free of charge, but you must provide battery details via a "Special Request Form" at least 48 hours before departure. 
4. Changes & Cancellations
Flexibility: Depending on your fare, changes made more than 8 weeks before departure usually incur a fee (approx. €30-€40). Within 8 weeks, most standard tickets are non-changeable and non-refundable.
Name Corrections: Minor corrections to personal details (name, DOB) can often be made free of charge via the "Manage Booking" section

3. In-Flight Services & Dining
Buy-on-Board Menu: Corendon offers a "Fly Café" and "Fly Bistro" service where you can purchase snacks and beverages. Special meals (e.g., vegetarian or gluten-free) must be requested at least 24 to 72 hours before departure.
Pre-Ordered Hot Meals: It is not possible to order hot meals once on board; they must be pre-ordered online up to 72 hours before your flight.
Alcohol Policy: You are not permitted to consume alcoholic drinks that were not purchased on board.
Nut Allergies: If you have a nut allergy, inform the cabin crew during boarding; they will make an announcement to other passengers and suspend the sale of nut products for that flight. 
2. Health & Fitness to Fly
Medical Reports: If you appear unwell, the airline may require an English-language medical report to allow boarding.
Restricted Conditions: Travel is generally not permitted for those who have suffered a heart attack or stroke within the last 8 weeks, or for infants less than 8 days old.
Emergency Exit Seats: To sit in extra legroom or exit rows, you must be physically able to assist in an evacuation. These seats are restricted for those over 70 years old, those with limited mobility, or anyone under 16 years old.

Bicycles: Non-electric bicycles are permitted for a fee of approx. €52, but electric bikes are strictly prohibited due to battery safety. 
2. Passenger Rights & Compensation (EU261)
Delays: If your flight is delayed by 3+ hours and it is the airline's fault, you may be entitled to compensation ranging from €250 to €600, depending on the distance.
Cancellations: If notified less than 14 days before departure, you are entitled to a full refund or rebooking, plus potential compensation.
Duty of Care: For delays over 2 hours, the airline must provide "care," including vouchers for food/drinks and, if necessary, overnight accommodation.
Overbooking: If you are denied boarding against your will, you are entitled to immediate compensation and a choice between a refund or an alternative flight. 
3. In-Flight Rules & Duty Free
Duty-Free Liquids: Items like perfume or alcohol purchased at the airport must remain in a sealed security bag with the receipt visible. If the seal is broken before your final destination, the items may be confiscated at transfer points.
Onboard Payments: Corendon typically operates as a cashless cabin on many routes; ensure you have a valid debit or credit card for onboard Fly Café purchases. 
4. Fare Specifics (2026 Update)
Promo/Lite Restrictions: These fares are generally non-refundable and non-changeable. If you do not show up, only the airport taxes (minus a €20 admin fee) are refundable.
Flex/Premium Benefits: Premium tickets include free seat selection and hot meals on most routes. Flex allows date changes more than 7 days before departure for a service fee (approx. €15) plus any fare difference.
